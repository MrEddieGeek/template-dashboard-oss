## Design Context

### Users
Ops analysts who spend long sessions inside the dashboard inspecting KPIs, drilling into time-series data, and triaging anomalies. They live in the product — not glance-and-leave executives — so density, scan-ability, and stable layouts matter more than first-impression flourish. Used in office settings on desktops/laptops; tablet/mobile is secondary but should not break.

### Brand Personality
Three words: **precise, instrumented, opinionated.** The product should feel like a piece of professional analytical equipment — closer to a Bloomberg terminal, a Linear/Vercel ops console, or a measurement instrument than a marketing landing page. Calm under load, dense without being noisy, confident in its choices.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Anti-references (explicit):** generic ChatGPT/Claude-generated dashboards, indigo→violet hero gradients, gradient text, glassmorphism, "AI palette" (cyan accents on dark), hero metric template (giant number + tiny label + sparkline trio), Inter/Plus Jakarta defaults.
- **Direction:** intentional, restrained, slightly editorial. Tabular figures everywhere numbers appear. Sharp typography with real hierarchy contrast (a distinctive non-default sans or grotesque, paired with a refined body face — picked from Klim, Pangram Pangram, Dinamo, or similar foundries, not the reflex list). Tinted neutrals (warm or slightly green-leaning, NOT blue) so the surface reads as a working tool, not a marketing site.
- **Theme:** both light and dark, built on OKLCH tokens with proper chroma reduction at lightness extremes. Dark mode is the working default for analyst sessions; light mode must be equally polished, not an afterthought.
- **Palette posture:** the current indigo/violet/cyan starter palette is up for replacement. Prefer one dominant tinted neutral system + one restrained accent (used for selection/positive/negative states only). 60-30-10 weight, accent rare.

### Design Principles
1. **Density with rhythm.** Pack information, but vary spacing to create groupings — never the same padding everywhere.
2. **Numbers are first-class.** Tabular figures, right-aligned in tables, deliberate decimal precision, deltas with sign and unit.
3. **Surfaces, not cards.** Flatten the hierarchy. Avoid the card-grid template; use rules, indentation, and whitespace to separate regions.
4. **Restraint over decoration.** No gradient text, no side-stripe borders, no glow accents, no decorative sparklines. Every visual element earns its place.
5. **Distinctive typography.** Pick fonts that signal "instrument," not "startup template." Reject Inter, Plus Jakarta, DM Sans, Space Grotesk, IBM Plex on sight.
